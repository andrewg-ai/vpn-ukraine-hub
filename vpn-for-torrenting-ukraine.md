## What Makes a Good Torrenting VPN

**Kill switch (essential):** If VPN drops mid-download, kill switch immediately blocks all internet traffic — preventing IP exposure. System-level kill switch (blocks all internet) is safer than app-level (blocks only specific apps).

**P2P-friendly servers:** Some VPN providers route all P2P traffic through dedicated servers optimized for high throughput. Others allow P2P on all servers.

**No-logs policy:** A VPN that keeps connection logs can potentially identify which user was assigned which IP at what time — defeating the privacy purpose.

**Port forwarding (optional):** Improves download speeds and seeding ability. Available on PIA and a few others.

**Speed:** Torrenting benefits from fast upload and download speeds to reach full P2P potential.

## VPN Configuration for qBittorrent (Most Used in Ukraine)

### Step 1: Enable kill switch in VPN app
Settings → Kill switch → System-level (preferred) or App-level

### Step 2: Configure qBittorrent to bind to VPN interface
qBittorrent → Tools → Options → Advanced → Network interface → Select VPN adapter (usually "tun0" on Windows or named after VPN)

This ensures qBittorrent **only** connects through VPN. If VPN drops, qBittorrent cannot connect at all — IP never exposed even if kill switch fails.

### Step 3 (Optional): Add SOCKS5 proxy in qBittorrent
For NordVPN or PIA users:
qBittorrent → Tools → Options → Connection → Proxy → Type: SOCKS5 → Enter VPN proxy details

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is torrenting legal in Ukraine?**
A: Torrenting itself is legal. Downloading or sharing copyrighted content without authorization is a copyright violation under Ukrainian law. VPN use is legal in Ukraine. This guide is for legal use cases — downloading open-source software, Creative Commons content, public domain works, and content you own.

**Q: Will my ISP know I'm torrenting with a VPN?**
A: With a quality VPN (NordVPN, Surfshark, PIA), your ISP sees only encrypted traffic to a VPN server — not P2P traffic or what you're downloading. Your ISP cannot identify torrenting activity.

**Q: What happens if my VPN disconnects while torrenting?**
A: With kill switch enabled, all internet traffic stops immediately when VPN drops. Your torrent client cannot send or receive data — your real IP is never exposed. Without kill switch, your real IP would appear in the swarm for seconds until VPN reconnects.

**Q: Does port forwarding really improve torrent speeds?**
A: Yes significantly, especially on Ukrainian residential connections with CGNAT. Port forwarding allows incoming peer connections — peers can find and connect to you rather than only you connecting to them. PIA is the only major VPN with free port forwarding on all plans.
